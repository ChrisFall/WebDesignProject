Project Discription


Introduction

In this project, we created software that allows a user to take a historically accurate virtual tour of the Stephen and Harriet Myers’ Residence, a house that was a headquarters for Underground Railroad activity in Albany, NY in the 1850s. To accomplish this, we collaborated with Siena history and creative arts students and learned to use 3D modeling and game development tools.

Methodology

Our workflow involved three stages. First, we coordinated with history students, who provided us with background information and reference photos of period appropriate furniture. We modeled the suggested furniture using Blender, imported our models into Unreal Engine 4.1, and programmed it to allow user interaction.

Modeling

Blender is open source 3D modeling software. We used it to sculpt models (polygonal meshes) of furniture and other items to populate the scene

Creating 3D World

Unreal Engine 4 (UE4) is a game engine used widely throughout the gaming industry. We constructed a centimeter accutate recreation of the Myers' Residence using UE4 geometry, then imported our Blender models and positioned them according to the recommendations from our history department collaborators.

Applying Materials

In UE4, materials are applied to meshes to give them lifelike detailincluding color, specular reflections, and opacity. It may include texture mapping which “paints” an image onto the mesh, and bump mapping which simulates bumps and wrinkles on the surface.

Blueprints

The Blueprint system in UE4 is a visual representation of the C++ programming language that uses nodes instead of written code. These are used to implement interactive elements such as movement, user interfaces, and physics objects

Virtual Reality

We worked with the Oculus Rift VR system.This includes a headset, two sensors, and two motion controllers, which could be used to pick up objects in the scene, which was enabled by UE4 Blueprints.

Future Work

This project may be expanded upon in the future by modeling additional floors, adding sound, improving the user interface, adding an outside view, acquiring/creating more textures/ materials, and adding animations to doors, drawers, etc…
